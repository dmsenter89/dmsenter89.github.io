I wrote a blog post for [blogs.sas.com](https://blogs.sas.com/content/) that went
live yesterday. This post walks you through how to run a Bayesian regression model
with a multiply imputed data set such as those produced by PROC MI. What I like about
this process is that you can get started working with a Bayesian model that includes
missing data even if you're not familiar with joint modeling techniques. It's pretty neat
and allows for knowledge transfer between your frequentist and Bayesian methods.

Go over to SAS to read my article entitled "[Getting started performing a Bayesian analysis with missing data in SAS
](https://blogs.sas.com/content/subconsciousmusings/2025/02/17/getting-started-performing-a-bayesian-analysis-with-missing-data-in-sas/)."
