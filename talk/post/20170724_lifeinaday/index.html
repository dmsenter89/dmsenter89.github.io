Life in a Day - Anniversary

Mon, Jul 24, 2017 movies

July 24th, 2010 was a normal day for most people. They got up in the morning, brushed their teeth and went about their business. For some, it may have been the best day of their life. For others, the worst. Some 6.9 billion people were alive on that day. Each with their own story, their own fears, their own dreams and challenges. Some were lucky and spent the day driving their sports car for fun or relaxing on a yacht. Others desperately tried to find food.

But there is one little fact that makes this day special. People around the world filmed their day. Not in the way we take snapshots with our cellphones for ourselves. They filmed in order to share their life with the world. In 2010 one of my favorite directors, Ridley Scott, anounced a collaboration with Scott Free Productions to create a very unique documentary. They would encourage people all over the world to film their day. To make sure even the poorest countries could participate, they arranged funds to send cameras to poor nations.

And so thousands of people from 192 countries created footage of July 24th, 2010. The producers went over all the videos and collected them into a “day.” It starts with footage from when it’s still dark in the wee-hours of the morning. It goes through the day until midnight. Despite being crowd-sourced it fits together surprisingly well. I love the movie because it shows that even though people around the world do live each in their own little worlds with distinct language, dress, food, and culture, we all share a common humanity that is often lost. It’s difficutl to realize the complextity inherent in all strangers and we often take mental shortcuts when dealing with people. To me, this movie gives hope for humanity. It gives me hope that we can all work together to provide a better world for ourselves and our descendents. I highly recommend watching this movie, especially if you haven’t seen it before. I really hope we can make a 25th or 50th anniversary edition in the future. It would be fun to compare how life has changed by then.
